![[Pasted image 20260324131206.png]]Figure 3. Shifting climate influences on rice yield over time. (a) Temporal evolution of correlation coefficient (r) and statistical significance (p-value) between April precipitation and rice yield from 1997 to 2019, showing a transition from negative to significantly positive correlation starting in 2013. (b) Temporal evolution of correlation between May salinity and rice yield, showing a shift from weakly positive to significantly negative correlation beginning in 2011. (c) Threshold analysis of salinity's impact on rice yield, showing increased negative correlation strength and statistical significance as lower salinity values are excluded. The bars represent the range of included salinity values, and the x-axis indicates the number of data points used in each analysis, revealing a threshold of 1552 ppm above which salinity significantly impacts rice yield.
